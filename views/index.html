Our Objective

At AquaAid, we are committed to raising awareness about the importance of water conservation and protecting aquatic ecosystems in India. Our mission is to educate the public, promote sustainable practices, and drive real change through community engagement and conservation initiatives.

Join us in our journey to make water accessible to all while ensuring the preservation of life below water. Together, we can protect our most precious resource and help secure a sustainable future for generations to come.

Facts About Indian Water Bodies and Marine Life

India's Vast Coastline

India has a coastline stretching over 7,500 km, supporting diverse marine ecosystems including mangroves and coral reefs.

The Ganges River System

The Ganges spans 2,500 km and supports over 400 million people, including the endangered river dolphin.

Coral Reefs of India

Coral reefs in Lakshadweep and Andaman support thousands of marine species and protect the ecosystem.

Sundarbans Mangrove Forest

The world’s largest mangrove forest, home to Bengal tigers, crocodiles, and unique aquatic species.

Indian Marine Biodiversity

India’s waters harbor over 1,200 fish species critical to marine health and the fishing industry.

Sea Turtle Nesting Grounds

Odisha’s Gahirmatha Beach is one of the largest nesting grounds for Olive Ridley sea turtles.
