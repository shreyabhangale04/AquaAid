Facts About Indian Water Bodies and Marine Life

India's Vast Coastline

India has a coastline stretching over 7,500 kilometers, bordering the Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and the Indian Ocean. This coastline supports diverse marine ecosystems including mangroves, coral reefs, and more.

The Ganges River System

The Ganges River spans over 2,500 kilometers and sustains over 400 million people. It is also home to the endangered Ganges river dolphin, one of the rarest marine mammals in the world.

Coral Reefs of India

India’s coral reefs, found in places like the Lakshadweep and Andaman Islands, are among the most productive marine ecosystems, providing shelter to thousands of marine species.

Sundarbans Mangrove Forest

The Sundarbans is the largest tidal halophytic mangrove forest in the world, home to unique species such as the Bengal tiger, estuarine crocodiles, and a variety of fish species.

Indian Marine Biodiversity

India’s marine waters harbor over 1,200 species of fish, including commercially important species such as tuna, mackerel, and sardines, which play a crucial role in the fishing industry.

Largest Sea Turtle Nesting Grounds

The Gahirmatha Beach in Odisha is one of the largest nesting grounds for Olive Ridley sea turtles, which arrive here for mass nesting events known as “arribadas.”
