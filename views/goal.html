Our Conservation Goals for India's Coral Reefs and Marine Life

Protecting Oceans, Preserving Life.

Top Our Goals

At the heart of our mission lies the preservation of India's precious coral reefs and marine ecosystems. Through sustainable actions, climate advocacy, and community involvement, we aim to protect and restore these vital habitats for future generations.

Reduce Carbon Emissions

Address climate change by lowering greenhouse gas emissions, as rising ocean temperatures and acidification pose significant threats to coral reefs.

Promote Sustainable Fishing

Implement regulations and practices that prevent overfishing and destructive fishing methods, which can harm coral ecosystems and deplete marine species.

Reduce Pollution and Coastal Runoff

Implement stricter pollution controls to reduce plastic, chemical, and agricultural runoff, which threaten coral reefs and marine life in Indiaâ€™s coastal areas.

Support Scientific Research and Monitoring

Promote ongoing research and monitoring programs to study coral reefs, marine biodiversity, and the effects of human activities on marine ecosystems, to inform future conservation efforts.

Strengthen Marine Protected Areas (MPAs)

Work with the government to expand and enforce Marine Protected Areas in key biodiversity hotspots to safeguard fragile coral ecosystems and endangered marine species.

Promote Climate Change Mitigation

Advocate for policies and actions that reduce carbon emissions, support renewable energy, and combat global warming, to protect marine ecosystems.
